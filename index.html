Magnify, is an app to help students to view delicate objects without having to touch them and in turn damage them in the process. The point of the app is to view the object in an Augmented Reality or AR for short, with this technology students will be able to view the object without having to touch them and damage them. This app will also include bits of information about the objects, it will show full detail of the materials that were used to create the object as well as historic information and their design process.It has a built in cloud sotrage where it stores the information that it has seen and stores it inside the cloud, while the student is in a no connection zone, they will be able to view and go through the saved files and check out their previous viewings.


Overview

The app runs through a profile system, where the students create a profile to be able to use the app. The profile system allows the student to store saved information on their profile so that they may be able to view them without any connections. During a non connection while using the app. The app will tap into the phone storage system where the cache that the app leaves behind will be loaded up showing the stored files that the student saved.

AR

The app runs in an AR system where the students can freely look at photogrammetry/3D model of the models. They can be interacted with a rotation system by swipping with your fingers, its in full 360 rotation and students can also zoom in to see the finer details.

Information

The app also includes the information about the models and the materials that the designers used to create them. The information will be taken from books in the library and short snippets of key statements will be stored into the app.


Categorize

The app will arrange each model so that users will not be confused while searching for the right model they want. It also shows different kinds of model styles as well as changing their color so that they will get a variation of choices. They are also able to change colours in AR mode so that they don't have to go through this process to change it.
